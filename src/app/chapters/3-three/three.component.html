Garrett and Kayla spent another day working on Grandpa Van Dubin’s house. Then, as they treated themselves to a pizza dinner, a tendril of smoke crept up from the basement. Garrett ran down and found a singed pentagram on the floor. It cracked and a hole formed.

“Kayla,” he called. She ran down the floor with Ignus and a fire extinguisher. The concrete burst. Ignus barked and Garrett stood next to him.

A leathery skinned creature, with tiny horns and long bat like ears, clawed out of the hole. Its eyes glittered like black granite. It smiled like a celebrity, and it wore a suit. Ignus bared his teeth.

“Pardon my intrusion, -sir, madam, canine- have I arrived at the home of Van Dubin?”

“Maybe,” said Garrett.

“Is there a Michael Van Dubin? Whose first born son still lives?”

“I’d be happy to give you an introduction,” said Garrett. His hands made fists. His heart pounded. The suited imp only grinned.

“Excellent,” said the creature, “My name on earth is Johnny McReaper. I’m here to collect your soul, Garrett.”

“Excuse me?” said Garrett.

“Go away creep!” said Kayla pointing the fire extinguisher.

“We have a contract for the first born of Michael Van Dubin,” said Johnny McReaper. The imp was unphased by a fire extinguisher. “Due to terms of entrance to our realm, and an incomplete quest.”

Garrett looked at the yellowed piece of paper. Its terms read for the promise of an oldest son, in crisp and unambiguous legal language.

“My master looks forward to meeting you at last,” said Johnny McReaper.

“I’m not going anywhere,” said Garrett, “now how about I beat you back into that hole?”

“The oldest son of Michael Van Dubin could no more harm me than the master himself. Well? Are you coming or not?”

“No!” said Garrett.

“Fine,” said McReaper. He raised his hands and Ignus was swept off his feet. Ignus barked and fell into the hole. McReaper jumped in leaving a puff of smoke behind.

“You know where to find us,” he jeered. “It’s the dog or you, first born of Van Dubin!”
